# Core Concepts

## Purpose

Core Concepts define the fundamental building blocks of Neuraverse. They establish a common vocabulary for designers, engineers, AI systems, and users, ensuring every feature is built upon consistent concepts and relationships.

## Domain

A Domain represents a broad area of knowledge, such as Artificial Intelligence, UX Design, Healthcare, or Climate Science. Domains organize conversations and help users discover communities of interest.

## Conversation

A Conversation is the primary knowledge object in Neuraverse. It starts with a question, topic, or problem and evolves over time through community participation.

## Perspective

A Perspective is an individual contribution to a conversation. It contains an opinion, experience, explanation, or recommendation and may be supported by evidence.

## Evidence

Evidence provides supporting material for a perspective, including research papers, articles, documents, datasets, images, links, or AI-discovered references.

## Debate

A Debate emerges when multiple perspectives present competing viewpoints. Debates help communities understand disagreements, compare evidence, and move toward better understanding.

## AI Summary

AI Summary continuously analyzes conversations to identify consensus, disagreements, emerging ideas, unanswered questions, and knowledge gaps.

## Knowledge Network

The Knowledge Network connects Domains, Conversations, Perspectives, Evidence, Debates, Experts, and AI Insights into an evolving graph of collective intelligence.

## Expert

Experts are contributors with recognized experience or validated expertise who help improve discussions through high-quality perspectives, evidence, and peer validation.

## Design Principles

- Conversations are living knowledge assets.
- Evidence strengthens trust.
- AI organizes rather than replaces human thinking.
- Every contribution should improve collective intelligence.
- Knowledge becomes more valuable as it evolves.
